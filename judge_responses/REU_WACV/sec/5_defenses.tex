\section{Can Safety Be Made Corruption-Robust?}
\label{sec:defenses}

\subsection{Safety tuning cracks under corruption}
\label{sec:defenses:tis}

Reasoning-based safety fine-tuning on Think-in-Safety data~\cite{tis2025} applied to LLaVA-CoT cuts SIUO ASR from 67.7\% (clean, undefended) to 25.1\% (clean, tuned) --- and then \emph{every one} of the ten corruptions claws part of it back, by $+1.3$ (motion blur) to $+8.4$ (zoom blur) points (sign test $p{=}0.002$). In relative terms the picture is stark: zoom blur costs the tuned model a third of its safety margin while costing it only 4 points of ScienceQA accuracy. Meanwhile the same tuned model is \emph{immune} to corruption exactly where the harm-location principle predicts: on typographic MM-SafetyBench its ASR is 0\% and on text-borne BeaverTails-style prompts ASR stays flat near 20\% (down from 77\%) under every corruption --- because there the safety trigger never depended on fragile visual evidence. Safety tuning, as currently practiced, hardens the text channel and leaves the image channel soft. It also carries a tax orthogonal to corruption: on benign prompts, over-refusal roughly doubles with TIS tuning, from 43.2\% to 82.3\% (mean over the XSTest~\cite{rottger2024xstest} safe subset, where refusals rise from 26.8\% to 74.8\% of 250 items, and a benign MM-SafetyBench-style suite, 59.6\% to 89.7\% of 428 items; scored with a conservative keyword refusal detector that if anything undercounts the tuned model's refusals, so these are lower bounds).

\subsection{Where does safety live? A module ablation}
\label{sec:defenses:cat}

\begin{table}[t]
  \centering
  \caption{\textbf{The final safety decision lives in the LLM, not the vision tower.} SIUO under zoom blur (severity 2), GPT-4o judge: reasoning-trace and conclusion ASR (\%) for the safety LoRA applied to different modules of LLaVA-CoT.}
  \label{tab:module}
  \small
  \begin{tabular}{lcc}
    \toprule
    Safety LoRA placement & ASR\textsubscript{reasoning} & ASR\textsubscript{conclusion} \\
    \midrule
    clean-trained, both (reference) & 72.5 & 31.1 \\
    corruption-trained, both        & 72.5 & 34.7 \\
    corruption-trained, LLM only    & 67.1 & 31.7 \\
    corruption-trained, vision only & \textbf{50.3} & 50.9 \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

Can training the safety tune \emph{on corrupted images} restore robustness? We retrain the safety LoRA on a 25/25/25/25 mix of clean/glass/snow/zoom images (the clean-only control reproduces the reference checkpoint's loss to the fourth decimal, 1.5930 vs.\ 1.5929, validating the pipeline). The verdict is split along the reasoning/answer axis: the reasoning trace becomes meaningfully safer (HR\textsubscript{R} $-5.6$) while the final answer does not (HR\textsubscript{C} $+2.4$).

The module ablation in \cref{tab:module} explains why, and is to our knowledge the first localization of corruption-robust safety within a VLM. Placing the corruption-aware LoRA only on the \emph{vision tower} produces the safest reasoning in the study (ASR\textsubscript{reasoning} 50.3 vs.\ 72.5 for the reference) --- adapting perception to corruption genuinely helps the model \emph{recognize} risk in degraded images --- yet its conclusion ASR is the \emph{worst} (50.9): the safer perception never reaches the final answer. Conversely, the LLM-only variant carries essentially all of the conclusion-level safety (31.7, matching the reference 31.1). The safety \emph{decision} is implemented in the language model; the vision tower only supplies its trigger. This decomposition sharpens the open problem: corruption-aware training fixes the trigger, but the decision pathway from a risk-aware reasoning trace to a refusal is itself the weakest link --- consistent with the reasoning-leakage finding of \cref{sec:results:reasoning}.

\subsection{Corruption-aware prompting: cheap and effective}
\label{sec:defenses:prompt}

\begin{table}[t]
  \centering
  \caption{\textbf{A corruption-aware system prompt recovers most of the lost safety at zero training cost.} SIUO HR\textsubscript{C} (\%) for LLaVA-CoT; lower is safer. \emph{blur-safe} = generic safety nudge $+$ explicit warning that the image may be degraded. The blur-safe prompt beats the generic prompt in all four conditions and drives every corrupted condition below the undefended \emph{clean} baseline (68.3).}
  \label{tab:prompting}
  \small
  \begin{tabular}{lccc}
    \toprule
    Condition & no prompt & safety & blur-safe \\
    \midrule
    clean      & 68.3 & 59.9 & \textbf{52.1} \\
    zoom blur  & 74.8 & 62.3 & \textbf{53.9} \\
    snow       & 71.9 & 58.7 & \textbf{50.9} \\
    glass blur & 71.3 & 61.7 & \textbf{56.9} \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

A system prompt that tells the model to check safety --- and that the image may be corrupted --- requires no training, no data, and no weight access. \Cref{tab:prompting} shows it is the strongest defense in this study: 15--21 points of HR\textsubscript{C} over no defense, a consistent 5--8 points over the corruption-agnostic safety prompt, and corrupted-input safety better than the undefended model achieves on \emph{clean} input. Warning the model that its visual evidence may be unreliable changes how it resolves ambiguity --- precisely the failure mode of \cref{sec:results:harmloc}, where corrupted evidence was resolved as ``unrelated to the image.'' The utility cost is negligible: MathVista moves 53.5$\rightarrow$53.0 and ScienceQA 90.8$\rightarrow$94.0 across the same prompts. \subsection{Limitations}
\label{sec:defenses:limits}

Our headline metrics flow through a GPT-4o judge; we mitigate with paired statistics, a clean-image Llama-Guard judge for FigStep, and \TODO{a human-agreement audit on $\sim$50 items (run this)}, but judge bias cannot be fully excluded. SIUO is small ($n{=}167$); we therefore never interpret single cells, and the $n{=}476$ HoliSafe replication \TODO{pending} carries the load-bearing universality claim. The corruption-aware fine-tuning result is one recipe (LoRA $r{=}8$, one mixing ratio) on one model, and the prompting defense is evaluated on one model. Finally, all evaluations use synthetic ImageNet-C corruptions; real captured degradations (motion at capture time, sensor noise, compression stacks) are correlated differently and deserve a dedicated benchmark.
