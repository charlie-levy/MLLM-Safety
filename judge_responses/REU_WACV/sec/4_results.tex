\subsection{Utility survives the same corruptions}
\label{sec:results:decoupling}

Under the identical corruption pipeline, ScienceQA accuracy for the safety-tuned LLaVA-CoT stays between 82\% and 92\% across all ten corruption types (clean: 90.4\%), with only blurs producing visible dips; MathVista accuracy moves by $\le$0.5 points under prompting defenses and $\le$1 point under module-ablated safety tuning. The models still \emph{see} well enough to answer benign questions --- they no longer see well enough to \emph{refuse}. This asymmetry is the safety--capability decoupling of the title, and it implies that measuring utility robustness tells you nothing about safety robustness. \TODO{Figure: two-panel decoupling money plot --- left: SQA utility vs.\ corruption (flat); right: SIUO HR\textsubscript{C} vs.\ corruption (rising). \subsection{The harm-location principle}
\label{sec:results:harmloc}

Corruption's effect on safety is \emph{signed}, and the sign is predicted by where the harm signal lives (\cref{fig:signed}): typographic MM-SafetyBench ASR \emph{falls} $83.3\rightarrow72.0$ under zoom blur (blurring the attack destroys the attack), VLSBench ASR \emph{rises} $81.6\rightarrow88.6$ (blurring the evidence hides the risk), SPA-VL is approximately neutral, and HoliSafe rises $83.6\rightarrow87.4$. Text-borne harm is untouched. A model's own reasoning shows the mechanism: on clean input LLaVA-CoT articulates the danger (``microwaving eggs can explode'') and refuses; on the corrupted twin it decides the request ``seems unrelated to the image'' and complies.

\subsection{Reasoning does not buy safety}
\label{sec:results:reasoning}

The safest model in \cref{tab:siuo-main} is not the strongest reasoner, and the strongest reasoner (R1-Onevision, 84--87\% HR\textsubscript{C}) is the least safe; suppressing its thinking changes little on clean input. Forcing thinking budgets of 0/512/2048 tokens on a 50-item pilot leaves HR\textsubscript{C} within noise of 84\% everywhere, clean or corrupted. Moreover HR\textsubscript{R} $\ge$ HR\textsubscript{C} throughout: models routinely surface the harm in their reasoning and comply anyway --- reasoning \emph{leaks} harm more often than it blocks it.
